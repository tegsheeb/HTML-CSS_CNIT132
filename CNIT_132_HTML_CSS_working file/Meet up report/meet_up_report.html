CNIT-132 Meetup report

I have participated meetup event called Algorithms & Interview Prep @ Strava on Nov 21, 2019 and the event was organized by public group "Women Who Code SF".

Objective of Women Who Code SF meetup group

Women Who Code SF is dedicated to providing an empowering experience for everyone who participates in or supports their community.

Women Who Code is the largest and most active community of engineers dedicated to inspiring women to excel in technology careers. Women Who Code envisions a world where women are representative as technical executives, founders, VCs, board members, and software engineers.

General information about the Women Who Code SF

Women Who Code SF usually holds event twice a week. One event is Women's Javascript Study Group which is on Tuesdays every week. Another event is Algorithms & Interview Prep on Thursdays every week, as well.

In general, the group's events offer free hands on technical events, study groups, panel discussions, lightning talks, and keynotes featuring influential tech industry experts, innovators, and investors. They help the group members to build the skills we need to raise our professional profile and achieve greater career success.

More about Algorithms & Interview Prep @ Strava and what I have learnt

The event I have attended on Thursday Nov 21, 2019 was about Binary trees and data Structure. At the event, I have met fellow CNIT-132 student Hazem, too. The event's schedule were as following:

6:30pm Welcome from event host and event leads
6:45pm Presentation of A StardustTruce by Chi and Sorrel (fellow group members. They introduced their product)
7:00pm Whiteboarding & Algorithm Prep in small groups, Subject is Binary tree and data structure
8:30pm End of event

What I have learnt from the event

For me binary tree was a new concept. So, it was great opportunity to learn new topic with fellow group members. We discussed, asked questions from each other. I think it was very good stimulating environment to learn new topic.
Also, it was first time to work on the whiteboard which is valuable experience, as well.
